Q 1.7: Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

A 1.7: The way GDPR impacts digital projects is as follows:

1) Lawful, fair and transparent processing: You must always process data in a lawful manner. Only use data for legitimate reasons and be completely transparent in how you will use the data you collect.

2) Limitation of purpose, data and storage: You must limit the use of data and collect only which you see as necessary to fulfil your requirement that is allowed by law. Forbid sharing of data without stating that you are going to do so in the first place and make sure data is deleted upon completion of the task in which it was collected for.

3)Data subject rights: The subjects in which the data has been collected from know their rights. Know they have the right to complain, correct and ask for deletion or transfer of their data.

4) consent: You must always get consent for any data you collect from the subject and if the subject is below the age of 16 that you have the consent of a relevant parent/guardian.

5)Personal data breaches: You must keep a register of all personal data breaches based on severity, the regulator and the subject of the data breach should be notified within 72 hours.

6)Privacy by design: You should incorporate organisational and technical mechanisms to protect user data that has been collected. This should be ensured by default.

7)Data protection Impact assessment: Data protection impact assessments should be conducted when you start a new project, initiate a change or make a or product.

8) Data transfers: You must always ensure the protection of data even when transferring to a third party. As the controller of the data you are responsible for its security.

9)Data protection officer: Where possible and as good practice you should always assign a data protection officer who would be responsible for advising the company about compliance with EU GDPR requirements etc

10)Awareness and training: You must ensure that you promote awareness within your company of GDPR requirements etc.
