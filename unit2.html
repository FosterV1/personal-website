Q 1.5: Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

A 1.5: Some of the constraints of copyright include what I can put on my website, but it also in turn protects and content that I put on my site that is original to me. constraints effecting websites can be what the end user is viewing your site on i.e. how it looks on a PC to how it looks on a phone.

Q 1.6: Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can overcome them

A 1.6: Issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages range from navigation problems to filling out forms and not being able to see relevant images. We can overcome these problems by making sure our webpage is navigable by using just the keyboard for instance, or adding text to images so that people who use screen readers can “see” the images too by having the description read out tot them.

Q 1.7: Describe which are the appropriate filetypes for websites

A 1.7: Appropriate file types for websites could be PNG, BMP, GIF, JPG and TIF for images. The choice of which of these you use can be anything from quality to size constraints on the webpage. TIF for instance is of the highest quality and maybe a lower quality file type could be used, such as JPG.
